I. Childhood Years

(2001-2012)

          1Just 20 years ago, Martin Angelo M. Ortega came in this world as a second-born child in his family. 2He grew up in the province of Morong, Rizal where 90% of his heart belongs to. 3For him, growing up in a small province is one of the nicest things a child will ever experience. 4He would go to the market with his mom or sometimes with his grandmother, every sunday. 5Whenever they go off to somewhere they would always come across with someone they know, either their classmate, friend, or your teacher, it is because the province is just small so you will often meet someone you know in the bakery while buying malunggay flavored pandesal.6Growing up, he also experienced playing with his friends along the street, they would go out in the afternoon to play marbles, teks, tilador, hampasan ng tsinelas, duraan, and asaran ng nanay. 7Martin is not an honor student, he never got an award in their class, only the mabuting bata award, because he returned a missing 20 pesos bill to the school guidance. 8His parents does not push him to be an honor student, so he became a consistent lazy student instead. 9One of his childhood fears is the fear of spiders. 10He always remember when his classmate brought a spider in school, the spider ran towards his arm and ended up stepping on the spider out of panic. 11One of his favorite family bonding during his childhood is when they go out to watch cinema almost every month. 12They would also go to his grandparents' house in Cubao during Christmas vacation and summer. 13During the Christmas vacation they would go to Araneta center to see the giant Christmas tree and watch the fireworks at night. 14Sometimes, they go to La Union for vacation along with their other relatives. 15His grandmother owns a house at Bauang, La Union, which gives them the chance to stay their for like a week. 16The house is just minutes away from the beach so they often go to the beach in the morning and afternoon to relax and have fun. 17 They would buy freshly catched fish from the fishermen and grill it for their lunch. 18Collecting shells at the beach is Martin's favorite thing to do, he would fill up a plastic cup with shells and then throw it back at the sea before they go home. 19During his childhood days, he does not actually care about everything, he just enjoys everything just like what young kids do. 20 For him, childhood days is the happiest phase of life, because as a child the only problem you would have is how you will explain to your mother that you lost her tupperware or foldable umbrella.
